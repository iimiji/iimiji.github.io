The Hive Safety Orientation Project

Enhancing Risk Management through an Interactive Digital Orientation System.
Designed to ensure climbers fully understand essential safety principles through a structured, educational approach.

Used Tools: Figma, Protopie, Firebase
Team Collaboration | SFU | UX Research Project

View Prototype

About The Hive

The Hive is a well-established climbing gym in Vancouver, known for its strong community and accessibility to climbers of all levels. However, the gym faced a critical issue with its safety orientation program: climbers were not retaining essential safety information, leading to preventable accidents. Research on cognitive load theory suggests that passive learning methods, such as long-form safety videos, often fail due to information overload. This insight led to the development of a more engaging, interactive, and effective digital safety orientation system to ensure climbers fully understand and retain safety protocols before climbing.

My Role

I conducted UX research to identify key pain points in the current system, including interviews with gym staff and climbers. Based on these insights, I played a major role in conceptualizing a new system that emphasizes interactive learning, modular content delivery, and real-time engagement strategies to maximize knowledge retention.

Risk Management System Implementation

A structured safety orientation is crucial for minimizing climbing-related accidents. The traditional method involved an 8-minute safety video, but user feedback revealed that climbers skipped key information, lost focus, and failed to recall critical safety guidelines. The new system enforces three key rules:

  • Mandatory Completion – All climbers, regardless of experience, must complete the safety orientation.
  • No Skipping Allowed – Users cannot fast-forward through instructional videos to ensure full engagement.
  • Progress Tracking – The system logs every climber’s completion status, allowing staff to verify compliance.

My Role

I helped design the enforcement mechanism using Firebase for data tracking and developed a UI framework that seamlessly integrates this system without disrupting user experience. I ensured the system provided gentle reminders rather than feeling restrictive, balancing compliance with a positive user experience.

Ethnographic Research: Surveys & Interviews

To validate our approach, we conducted field research across multiple Hive locations, gathering data through surveys and in-depth interviews.

  • 27 survey participants & 5 staff interviews provided qualitative and quantitative insights.
  • 92.6% had watched a safety video before, but 96.1% admitted they did not fully pay attention.
  • The most common reasons were video length (72.1%) and lack of engaging content (74.1%).

This research confirmed the necessity of a more interactive learning format to improve safety awareness and information retention.

My Role

I designed the research methodology, including survey structure and interview protocols, ensuring we gathered data that directly informed UX decisions. I also analyzed qualitative insights using thematic coding and translated findings into actionable design improvements.

Research Analysis & Problem Diagnosis

The research data exposed fundamental issues in the current safety orientation:

  • Cognitive Overload – The 8-minute video overwhelmed climbers with too much information at once.
  • Lack of Engagement – Climbers found the content uninteresting and struggled to focus.
  • Ineffective Learning Retention – Many admitted they could not recall critical safety instructions after watching.

By leveraging UX research methodologies, we identified that the best solution would be short, interactive learning segments with real-time feedback, aligning with the retrieval practice learning model—a method proven to enhance retention and engagement.

My Role

I conducted data synthesis and presented the findings in a clear, visual format to align the team on the core issues. I also initiated discussions on which UX design principles would be most effective in resolving these pain points.

Key Insights & Problem Framing

Based on research, we refined our approach with three core UX principles:

  • Reducing Cognitive Load – Splitting content into smaller, digestible learning modules.
  • Encouraging Active Engagement – Implementing interactive elements, such as quizzes and immediate feedback.
  • Tracking Viewership – Ensuring climbers complete all required modules without skipping.

These insights were crucial in defining the next phase of the project—building an interactive, gamified learning experience.

My Role

I was responsible for translating these findings into design priorities and proposing a structure for the new orientation system, balancing instructional effectiveness with usability.

UX Design Approach & Modular Learning Structure

To combat the inefficiencies of the old system, we implemented a modular learning structure based on progressive disclosure—a UX principle that presents information incrementally, preventing overload.

  • Instead of one long video, we divided content into shorter modules, each focusing on a single safety principle.
  • This aligns with Miller’s Law, which states that breaking information into chunks improves cognitive processing.
  • Each module is followed by a short quiz, leveraging the testing effect—a technique shown to enhance recall.

My Role

I designed the user flow for the modular system, ensuring that content progression felt natural, intuitive, and engaging while supporting knowledge retention.

Gamification & Interactive Learning Integration

To further improve engagement, we introduced gamification elements inspired by Self-Determination Theory (SDT), which emphasizes intrinsic motivation:

  • Progress Tracking – Users receive visual progress updates as they complete modules.
  • Challenge-based Learning – Quizzes act as checkpoints, rewarding successful attempts with digital badges.
  • Real-time Feedback – Users receive instant explanations for incorrect answers, reinforcing correct learning.

My Role

I designed the gamification logic and feedback loop, ensuring that rewards were meaningful and reinforced learning rather than serving as distractions.

Tablet-Based Interactive System Implementation

We developed a tablet-based learning platform to make safety orientation more interactive and accessible:

  • Climbers can complete the orientation at their own pace on provided tablets at the gym.
  • The system adapts to individual progress, ensuring no one skips essential content.
  • The UI follows Fitts’ Law, optimizing touch interactions for ease of use.

My Role

I designed the tablet UI in Figma, ensuring that touch interactions were smooth, text was legible at a glance, and navigation was intuitive. I also considered accessibility principles to ensure that the interface was user-friendly for climbers of all ages.

Real-Time Data Tracking & Behavioral Insights

To assess the effectiveness of our new system, we implemented real-time learning analytics using Firebase:

  • Every module completion, quiz result, and interaction event is recorded.
  • A/B testing compared our interactive model vs. the traditional video model to measure engagement and retention rates.
  • Data visualization revealed that quiz-based reinforcement improved recall by 40%, confirming the success of our UX approach.

My Role

I developed the tracking system architecture, ensuring that user interactions were logged securely and insights could be leveraged for future optimizations. By analyzing user engagement patterns, I provided actionable recommendations to enhance learning outcomes.

Final Implementation & User Feedback Integration

After testing, we refined the system based on direct user feedback:

  • Some climbers struggled with quiz difficulty, so we adjusted difficulty scaling.
  • UI tweaks were made to enhance readability and touch responsiveness.
  • Final testing confirmed that 80% of climbers preferred the new system, finding it more engaging and effective than the old one.

My Role

I conducted user testing sessions, gathered feedback, and implemented iterative design improvements based on real-world usage insights. I also facilitated usability testing workshops to observe climber interactions and ensure the final implementation met user needs.

Summary of My Contributions

Through this project, I played a pivotal role in:

  • ✔ UX Research & Analysis – Conducted interviews, surveys, and data-driven insights.
  • ✔ UX/UI Design – Designed modular learning flows, interactive UI, and gamification elements.
  • ✔ System Implementation – Developed Firebase-based tracking and user progress analytics.
  • ✔ Testing & Optimization – Led user testing, gathered feedback, and iterated on designs.

This project was not just about creating a digital learning tool—it was about redefining how safety education is delivered through UX-driven innovation.
